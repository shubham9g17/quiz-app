## The Earliest People and Why They Travelled from one place to another

- In the early period, people used to gather food by hunting and gathering. They were known as hunter-gatherers. They hunted animals, caught fish and gathered plants to fulfil their food requirements.
- If they had stayed in one place for a long time, they would have eaten up all the available plant and animal resources from that region.
- Since animals move from place to place, these people had to follow their movements to hunt them.
- Plants and trees are dependent on seasons, so people had to move from season to season in search of various types of plants.
- People also travelled in search of water, because water was very important for the survival of plants, people and animals.

## Bhimbetka Caves
- The Bhimbetka rock shelters are an archaeological site in Madhya Pradesh that spans the prehistoric Palaeolithic and Mesolithic periods.
- *Palaeolithic* comes from two Greek words: 'Palaeo' meaning 'old' and 'lithos' meaning 'stone'. Hence, this period is also called the Old Stone Age.
- The period when we find environmental changes began about 12,000 years ago till about 10,000 years ago, is called the Mesolithic Middle Stone Age. Stone tools found during this period are generally tiny and are called microliths.
- Bhimbetka exhibits the earliest traces of human life in India.
- It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
- The Bhimbetka rock shelters were discovered by V. S. Wakankar in 1957.

## Rock Paintings
- The earliest people drew images and symbols on rock surfaces. The first prehistoric paintings were uncovered in Madhya Pradesh's Bhimbetka caves.
- Many of the caves where these early people lived have paintings on the walls.
- Some of the best examples are from Madhya Pradesh and southern Uttar Pradesh.

## Beginning of Farming and Herding
- Around 12,000 years ago, there were major changes in the world's climate, which led to the development of grasslands in many areas. The number of animals like deer, goat, sheep and cattle increased due to the increase in grasslands.
- People started thinking about herding and rearing these animals themselves. Fishing also became important.
- After the development of grasslands, people learned to grow wheat, rice and barley.
- People also started taming animals, and the first animal to be tamed was the wild ancestor of the dog. Later, they started domesticating sheep, goats and cattle as well. In this way, humans started herding.

## Beginning of a New Era
- People started living in the same place for a longer period to look after the plants until the grains ripened. They stored grains in large clay pots, wove baskets or dug pits into the ground for food and seeds.
- Animals were nurtured better now because they provided milk, meat, etc., as per the requirement. In other words, animals that are reared can be used as a 'store' of food.
- The earliest farmers and herders were found all over the subcontinent. The most important sites were found in the northwest, in present-day Kashmir and in eastern and southern India.

## A Settled Life
- Traces of huts, houses were also found by archaeologists in different parts of the subcontinent. Stone-built houses in Kashmir.
- Stone tools have been found from many sites. The tools belonging to the Neolithic age were different from those of the Palaeolithic age.
- The tools of the Neolithic age were polished and had accurate cutting edges. Some tools were made of stone that belonged to the Palaeolithic age.
- Many kinds of earthen pots have also been found. People used these pots to store things and cook food.
- People also began weaving cloth, using different kinds of materials, such as cotton.

## Living and Dying in Mehrgarh
- Mehrgarh is a Neolithic archaeological site situated in Balochistan, Pakistan. It is located near the Bolan Pass, which is one of the most important routes from Central Asia into India past the Bolan Pass and on to Iran.
- Mehrgarh was one of the places where people learned to grow barley and wheat and rear sheep and goats for the first time in this area. In this village, many animal bones were found.
- When people died, they were buried with goats, which were probably meant to serve as food in the next world. This suggests that they believed in some form of life after death.
