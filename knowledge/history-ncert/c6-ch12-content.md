## The Iron Pillar
- Ancient Indian metallurgists made major contributions to the metallurgical history of the world. Archaeological excavations have shown that the Harappans were masters of copper metallurgy. They even manufactured bronze by mixing copper and tin. While the Harappans belonged to the Bronze Age, their successors belonged to the Iron Age. India produced highly advanced types of iron-forged iron, wrought iron and cast iron.
- The iron pillar at Mehrauli, Delhi, was made about 1,500 years ago, showing the outstanding skill of Indian craftspersons. It is made of iron, 7.2 metre high and weighs over 3 tonnes.
- The date has been inscribed on the pillar mentioning a ruler named Chandra, who probably belonged to the Gupta dynasty. One interesting fact of the pillar is that it has not rusted in all these years.

## Buildings in Brick and Stone

### Stupas
- A stupa is a mound-like or hemispherical structure containing relics, used as a place of meditation. Stupas can vary in shape and size—some round and tall, others big and small—but they share certain common features.
- At the centre of the stupa, a small box was placed, which contained bodily remains (such as teeth, bone or ashes) of the Buddha or his followers, or items they used, as well as precious stones and coins.
- The box was known as a relic casket and covered with a layer of brick. The dome-like structure was sometimes covered with carved stone slabs.
- Stupas were the places of worship and there was a path around the stupa, known as the pradakshina patha. Devotees walked around the stupa, in a clockwise direction, as a mark of devotion.
- Sanchi Stupa: The Sanchi Stupa is a UNESCO world heritage site since 1989. Sanchi is in Madhya Pradesh. It is considered one of the most spectacular monuments of its time.
- During this period, some of the earliest Hindu temples were also built. Deities such as Vishnu, Shiva and Durga were worshipped in these shrines.
- The most important part of the temple was the room known as the garbhagriha, where the image of the chief deity was placed. It was here that priests performed religious rituals and devotees offered worship to the deity.
- Bhitargaon, a tower, known as the shikhara, was built on top of the garbhagriha, to mark this out as a sacred place. Most temples also had a space known as the mandapa a hall where people could assemble.
- An early temple at Bhitargaon, Uttar Pradesh.
- This was built about 1,500 years ago and was made of baked brick and stone.
- This was carved out of a huge, single piece of rock (hence known as monoliths).
- While brick structures are built up by adding layers of bricks from the bottom upwards, in this case, the stone cutters had to work from the top downwards.

## Paintings

### Ajanta
- Ajanta is a place where several caves were hollowed out of the hills over centuries. Most of these were monasteries for Buddhist monks and some of them were decorated with paintings.
- The Ajanta Caves are approximately 30 rock-cut Buddhist cave monuments dating from the 2nd century BCE to about 480 CE, located in the Aurangabad district of Maharashtra, India.
- The walls of the Ajanta caves are decorated with Ajanta paintings, depicting the pomp and splendour of the royal courts, the romance of love, singing and dancing. Some of them depict the world of nature—vegetation and flowers, animals and birds.
- Many of the themes depicted are from the Buddha's life and Jataka stories. The vivid colours, which are bright even after 1,500 years, were made of plants and minerals. The artists who created these splendid works of art remain unknown.

## Recording and Preserving Old Stories
- A number of Hindu religious stories were written down around the same time. These include the Puranas, meaning old. The Puranas contain stories about gods and goddesses, such as Vishnu, Shiva, Durga and Parvati. They also contain details on how they were to be worshipped.
- The Puranas were written in simple Sanskrit verses and were meant to be heard by everybody, including women and Shudras, who were not allowed to study the Vedas. They were probably recited in temples by priests and people came to listen to them.
- Two Sanskrit epics, the Mahabharata and Ramayana, have been popular for a very long time. The Mahabharata is about a war fought between the Kauravas and Pandavas, who were cousins. The Ramayana is about Rama, a prince of Kosala, who was sent into exile. His wife Sita was abducted by the king of Lanka, named Ravana, and Rama had to fight a battle to get her back. Valmiki is the author of the Sanskrit Ramayana.

## Stories Told By Ordinary People
- Ordinary people also told stories, composed poems and songs, sang, danced and performed plays.
- Some of these are preserved in collections of stories such as the Jatakas and the Panchatantra, which were written down around this time.
- Stories from the Jatakas were often shown on the railings of stupas and in paintings in places such as Ajanta.
