## Oldest Books in the World
- The Vedas are a large body of religious texts originating in ancient India. Composed in Vedic Sanskrit, they represent oldest layer of Sanskrit literature and the oldest scriptures of Hinduism.
- There are four Vedas: the Rigveda, the Samaveda, the Yajurveda and the Atharvaveda.

## The Rigveda
- The Rigveda is the oldest Veda, composed around 1,500 BCE. It includes more than a thousand hymns, called suktas. Hymns were composed by sages (rishis). These hymns are in praise of various gods and goddesses, including:
  1. Agni, the god of fire.
  2. Indra, a warrior god.
  3. Soma, a plant from which a special drink was prepared.
- Most of these hymns were taught, composed and learned by men. Women composed a few of them too.
- This form of literature was taught by priests to students, who were made to recite and memorise them.
- The Rigveda is in Vedic or old Sanskrit. It was recited by the people and not just read. Written scripts were printed less than 100 years ago.

## People of Rigveda
- There are two groups of people mentioned in Rigveda:
- The Priest: Also called the Brahmins. They performed all the rituals of Hinduism.
- Rajas: Unlike the later kings, they did not have a palace, capital or army. They also do not levy taxes on the people of the state.
- Two words describe the people of Rigveda - Jana and Vish. (The word vaishya comes from vish.)
- The people who composed the hymns described themselves as Aryas and used to speak different languages.
- Those called their opponents Dasas or Dasyus. Later the term dasa (and the feminine dasi) came to mean slave. Slaves were women and men who were often captured in war.

## What are the Megaliths?
- The Megaliths are large stones found from the Rigvedic period, placed to mark cemeteries.
- This practice began more than 3000 years ago and was influential throughout southern India, the Deccan Plateau and Kashmir.
- They all shared a standard feature: the dead were buried with special pots called Red and Black Ware.

## Social Differences
Rich people were buried with more gold beads, stone beads, copper bangles, etc. Whereas poor people were buried with only a few pots.

## Importance of Burial Spots
People who belonged to the same family were often buried in the same place. Significant spots, such as boulders or stone circles, helped identify family burial sites.

## Inamgaon
- Inamgaon is a post-Harappan agrarian village and archaeological site located in the Pune district of Maharashtra.
- Occupation of the People of Inamgaon: Archaeologists have found various types of crops in the fields of Inamgaon. Some of them are sesame, peas, millets, pulses, rice, barley and wheat.
- Animals were also a source of food for the people as evidenced by cuts on bones.
- Several fruits were consumed by the people of Inamgaon, namely dates, jamun, amla and ber.
- A special burial at Inamgaon: This site on the river Ghod, a tributary of the Bhima, reveals that adults were buried in the ground—laid out straight with their heads towards the north. Vessels that probably contained food and water were placed with the deceased.
