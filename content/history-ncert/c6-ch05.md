## Level 01 | Moderate

### Q1. The term *Veda* is derived from which of the following Sanskrit roots?
- (a) Vaj
- (b) Dhr
- **(c) Vid** ✓
- (d) Bhu
> **Explanation:** The word Veda comes from the Sanskrit root *vid*, which means "knowledge." The four Vedas — Rig, Yajur, Sama, and Atharva — are considered the oldest texts in Indian tradition and were passed down orally with remarkable accuracy.

### Q2. Consider the following statements:
1. The Rig Veda is composed entirely in classical Sanskrit.
2. Indra and Agni are among the deities mentioned in the Rig Veda hymns.

Which of the above statements is/are correct?
- (a) 1 only
- **(b) 2 only** ✓
- (c) Both 1 and 2
- (d) Neither 1 nor 2
> **Explanation:** Statement 1 is incorrect. The Rig Veda is written in Vedic Sanskrit, which is older and more archaic than classical Sanskrit. Vedic Sanskrit has distinct grammatical and phonetic features that set it apart from the later standardised classical form. Statement 2 is correct. Indra (the god of thunder and war) and Agni (the fire god and divine messenger) are two of the most frequently invoked deities in the Rig Veda. In fact, the very first hymn of the Rig Veda is dedicated to Agni.

### Q3. Match the following Vedic philosophical terms with their meanings:
| A. Atman | 1. Cosmic law or order |
| B. Brahman | 2. Inner self |
| C. Ritam | 3. Ultimate divine reality |
| D. Karma | 4. Action and consequence |

Choose the correct option from the code given below:
- **(a) A-2, B-3, C-1, D-4** ✓
- (b) A-3, B-2, C-1, D-4
- (c) A-4, B-3, C-2, D-1
- (d) A-2, B-1, C-3, D-4
> **Explanation:** In the Upanishadic tradition: Atman refers to the inner self. Brahman is the universal divine reality. Ritam signifies the cosmic order. Karma means action and its consequence.

### Q4. Which of the following tribal deities is worshipped in central India?
- (a) Donyipolo
- **(b) Khandoba** ✓
- (c) Jagannath
- (d) Singbonga
> **Explanation:** Khandoba is revered in central India and is considered a tribal deity. Similarly, Donyipolo is worshipped in Arunachal Pradesh, Singbonga by Munda and Santhal tribes, and Jagannath, though now a part of mainstream Hinduism, is believed to have tribal origins in Odisha. These examples showcase the deep integration between tribal and mainstream religious traditions in India.

### Q5. The prayer "Sarve bhavantu sukhinah" ("May all beings be happy") reflects the ________ philosophy emerging from the Vedic Upanisadic tradition.
- (a) Ritualistic
- (b) Materialistic
- **(c) Cosmic Unity** ✓
- (d) Tribal
> **Explanation:** The Upanisadic view emphasised that Atman (self) is one with Brahman (universal reality), fostering universal harmony and cosmic unity, as reflected in prayers like *Sarve bhavantu sukhinah*.

## Level 02 | Advanced

### Q1. Arrange the following developments in chronological order:
1. Rise of Yoga as a spiritual path
2. Emergence of the Upanisads
3. Composition of the Rig Veda
4. Tribal versions of the Mahabharata in Northeast India

- (a) 1-2-3-4
- (b) 3-1-2-4
- (c) 2-1-3-4
- **(d) 3-2-1-4** ✓
> **Explanation:** Composition of the Rig Veda — Estimated between 1500–1100 BCE, this is the earliest known Vedic text, forming the foundation of Vedic literature. Emergence of the Upanisads — Composed roughly between 900–300 BCE, the Upanisads mark a shift toward philosophical and metaphysical inquiry within the Vedic tradition. Rise of Yoga as a spiritual path — While early references to yoga appear in the Upanisads and the Bhagavad Gita, the systematisation of yoga as a spiritual discipline is more clearly seen in texts like the Yoga Sutras of Patañjali (circa 2nd century BCE to 5th century CE). Tribal versions of the Mahabharata in Northeast India — These are regional oral traditions that likely developed much later, as the epic spread and was adapted across different cultures and geographies.

### Q2. Consider the following statements regarding oral traditions:
1. They are restricted to tribal societies alone.
2. They have influenced mainstream Hindu traditions over time.

Which of the statements given above is/are correct?
- (a) 1 only
- **(b) 2 only** ✓
- (c) Both 1 and 2
- (d) Neither 1 nor 2
> **Explanation:** Statement 1 is incorrect. While oral traditions are indeed rich and vibrant in tribal societies, they are not exclusive to them. Oral storytelling, chants, epics, and songs have been integral to many communities across India, including rural and urban populations, religious sects, and classical traditions. Statement 2 is correct. Oral traditions have deeply shaped Hindu practices, from the recitation of Vedic hymns to the regional retellings of epics like the Ramayana and Mahabharata. Many tribal deities and rituals have also been absorbed into the Hindu pantheon, creating a rich tapestry of shared beliefs and practices.

### Q3. With reference to Indian tribal groups, consider the following statements:
1. The term *jana* was used in ancient texts to denote them.
2. Many tribal communities worship a supreme deity.
3. Tribal religions have never adopted Hindu epics.
4. The Constitution of India recognises over 700 tribes.

How many of the statements given above are correct?
- (a) Only two
- **(b) Only three** ✓
- (c) All four
- (d) None
> **Explanation:** Statement 1 is correct. In the Rig Veda, *janas* referred to tribal or clan-based communities, often associated with specific regions and kinship ties. Statement 2 is correct. While tribal religions are often animistic, many also worship a supreme or high god, sometimes associated with nature or ancestral spirits. Statement 3 is incorrect. In fact, many tribal communities have adapted and localised versions of Hindu epics like the Ramayana and Mahabharata, blending them with their own oral traditions and cultural motifs. Statement 4 is correct. As per the latest official listings, the Constitution recognises over 700 Scheduled Tribes across various states and union territories.

### Q4. Consider the following Assertion and Reason:
Assertion (A): The Rig Veda has been preserved with remarkable accuracy across generations.
Reason (R): The Vedic hymns were transmitted orally through a disciplined tradition of memorisation.

Choose the correct option:
- **(a) A and R are true, and R is the correct explanation of A.** ✓
- (b) A and R are true, but R is not the correct explanation of A.
- (c) A is true, but R is false.
- (d) A is false, but R is true.
> **Explanation:** The Rig Veda has indeed been preserved with remarkable accuracy over millennia. This is largely due to the rigorous oral tradition followed by Vedic scholars, which included highly sophisticated memorisation techniques such as Padapatha, Krama Patha, Jata Patha, and Ghana Patha. These methods ensured not only the preservation of words but also their intonation, pronunciation, and sequence, making even minor deviations easily detectable.

### Q5. With reference to early Vedic society, consider the following statements:
1. All *janas* lived in the Gangetic plains during the Rig Vedic period.
2. The terms *sabha* and *samiti* indicate early forms of assemblies.
3. It was structured around large kin-based communities, often called *janas*.

Which of the statements given above is/are correct?
- (a) 1 and 2 only
- **(b) 2 and 3 only** ✓
- (c) 1 and 3 only
- (d) 1, 2 and 3
> **Explanation:** Statement 1 is incorrect. During the Rig Vedic period, most janas (tribes) lived in the northwestern region of the Indian subcontinent, particularly around the Saptasindhu (land of seven rivers), not the Gangetic plains. The Gangetic plains became more prominent in the later Vedic period. Statements 2 and 3 are correct. Both sabha and samiti were tribal assemblies mentioned in the Rig Veda. They played important roles in governance, decision-making, and advising the tribal chief or rajan. The early Vedic society was organised around kinship-based tribal units known as janas, which were mobile and pastoral in nature.
