## Level 01 | Moderate

### Q1. Consider the following features:
1. Large buildings and structured cities
2. Use of written language
3. Proper sanitation and drainage system
4. Skilled occupational groups like potters and metalworkers

Which of the above are associated with the Indus Valley Civilisation?
- (a) 1 and 2 only
- (b) 1, 2 and 3 only
- **(c) 1, 2, 3 and 4** ✓
- (d) 2 and 4 only
> **Explanation:** All the features listed—urban planning, writing system, sanitation, and skilled workers—are key elements of the Indus Valley Civilisation, also known as India's First Urbanisation.

### Q2. Which of the following is often referred to as India's 'Second Urbanisation'?
- (a) Growth of cities during the Gupta Empire
- (b) Urban revival during the Mauryan period
- **(c) Emergence of urban centres in the 1st millennium BCE in the Ganga plains** ✓
- (d) Urban development during the Mughal era
> **Explanation:** India's Second Urbanisation refers to the growth of cities in the Ganga plains and nearby areas during the 1st millennium BCE.

### Q3. Which of the following literary sources provide evidence of India's Second Urbanisation?
1. Vedic texts
2. Jain literature
3. Buddhist literature
4. Sangam literature

Select the correct answer using the code below:
- (a) 1 and 2 only
- **(b) 1, 2 and 3 only** ✓
- (c) 2, 3 and 4 only
- (d) 1, 3 and 4 only
> **Explanation:** The Second Urbanisation is described in Vedic, Buddhist, and Jain texts. Sangam literature pertains to southern India and is of a later period.

### Q4. With reference to urban development in ancient India, consider the following statements:
1. Urban life disappeared completely after the fall of the Harappan cities.
2. A few small towns might have existed in north India during the 1000-year gap between the First and Second Urbanisation.

Which of the above is/are correct?
- (a) 1 only
- **(b) 2 only** ✓
- (c) Both 1 and 2
- (d) Neither 1 nor 2
> **Explanation:** Statement 1 is incorrect—urban life almost disappeared, but not completely. Statement 2 is correct—there is evidence that a few small towns still existed in North India.

### Q5. The word 'Janapada' in Sanskrit literally means:
- (a) Land of kings
- (b) Fortified city
- **(c) Place where people have settled** ✓
- (d) Land of wealth
> **Explanation:** 'Janapada' comes from jana (people) and pada (foot/step), meaning the place place where people have set foot or settled.

### Q6. Which of the following Mahajanapadas was located in the northwest of the Indian subcontinent?
- (a) Avanti
- (b) Anga
- **(c) Gandhara** ✓
- (d) Kuru
> **Explanation:** Gandhara was situated in the northwest, in present-day Afghanistan and Pakistan.

### Q7. Which of the following correctly matches a mahajanapada with its region?
- (a) Anga – Central India
- (b) Gandhara – Eastern India
- **(c) Ashmaka – Near the Godavari River in Central India** ✓
- (d) Kosala – Northwestern India
> **Explanation:** Ashmaka was located in Central India, near the Godavari River. Anga was in the eastern region, not central. Gandhara was in the northwest, not east. Kosala was in North India, mainly in modern Uttar Pradesh, not northwest.

### Q8. Consider the following features of ancient Indian cities during the mahajanapada period:
1. Large in size and well-fortified
2. Surrounded by moats for additional protection
3. Wide open gates to allow free movement of people and goods
4. Many of these cities still exist today

Which of the above statements are correct?
- **(a) 1, 2 and 4 only** ✓
- (b) 1, 2 and 3 only
- (c) 2, 3 and 4 only
- (d) 1, 3 and 4 only
> **Explanation:** Statement 3 is incorrect: Gates were kept narrow to maintain security and control. All other statements are correct—the cities were large, fortified, had moats and many still exist as modern cities.

### Q9. Which of the following Mahajanapadas was situated near the Godavari River?
- (a) Kuru
- **(b) Ashmaka** ✓
- (c) Kosala
- (d) Magadha
> **Explanation:** Ashmaka was the only Mahajanapada located in southern India, near the Godavari River.

### Q10. Which one of the following is NOT one of the sixteen mahajanapadas mentioned in ancient texts?
- (a) Gandhara
- (b) Anga
- (c) Ashmaka
- **(d) Kanchipuram** ✓
> **Explanation:** Gandhara, Anga and Ashmaka were among the mahajanapadas. Kanchipuram was an important city in South India, but not one of the 16 mahajanapadas.

### Q11. In the political setup of a janapada, the sabha and samiti were:
- (a) Military officers
- (b) Administrative departments
- (c) Local taxes
- **(d) Councils for decision-making** ✓
> **Explanation:** Sabha and samiti were assemblies where elders discussed important issues. These terms appear in Vedic texts.

### Q12. What was the nature of kingship in most Mahajanapadas?
- (a) Elected annually by the people
- (b) Based on military rank
- **(c) Hereditary and monarchical** ✓
- (d) Based on wealth and status
> **Explanation:** In most Mahajanapadas, the raja's position was hereditary—typically passed from father to son.

### Q13. What was unique about the republican Mahajanapadas like Vajji?
- (a) They had no leadership
- **(b) Decisions were made by assemblies through discussion or voting** ✓
- (c) They followed the Buddhist order only
- (d) They used tribal warfare to rule
> **Explanation:** Republics like Vajji used assemblies (Sabha or samiti) to make decisions, even electing the raja.

### Q14. What is a key difference between monarchies and republics in the Mahajanapadas?
- (a) Republics had no taxation system
- (b) Monarchies had no cities
- **(c) In republics, power was shared and not hereditary** ✓
- (d) Monarchies followed Jainism, republics followed Buddhism
> **Explanation:** In republics (gana-sanghas), the ruler was elected or chosen, and power was shared, unlike in hereditary monarchies.

### Q15. Which of the following Mahajanapadas were powerful monarchies?
- **(a) Magadha, Kosala, Avanti** ✓
- (b) Vajji, Malla, Gandhara
- (c) Ashmaka, Kuru, Malla
- (d) Anga, Vajji, Panchala
> **Explanation:** Magadha, Kosala and Avanti were known for their strong monarchies and military strength.

## Level 02 | Advanced

### Q1. With reference to iron use in ancient India, consider the following statements:
1. Iron tools became widespread by the late 2nd millennium BCE.
2. Iron helped improve agriculture and warfare.
3. The Harappans were the first to use iron extensively.

Which of the above statements is/are correct?
- **(a) 1 and 2 only** ✓
- (b) 2 and 3 only
- (c) 1 and 3 only
- (d) 1, 2 and 3
> **Explanation:** Harappans used copper and bronze, not iron. Iron tools became common later and helped in farming and weapon-making.

### Q2. Consider the following statements about the punch-marked coins:
1. They were India's earliest coins.
2. They were made only of copper.
3. They had symbols punched into them.

Which of the statements given above is/are correct?
- (a) 1 and 2 only
- **(b) 1 and 3 only** ✓
- (c) 2 and 3 only
- (d) 1, 2 and 3
> **Explanation:** The first coins were made of silver, not just copper. They had symbols punched, hence called punch-marked coins.

### Q3. Consider the following statements about the spread of religious ideas during the Mahajanapada period:
1. Teachings of Buddhism and Jainism spread through monks and scholars.
2. These ideas were limited only to the ruling class.
3. Pilgrimage played a role in spreading religious knowledge.

Which of the statements is/are correct?
- (a) 1 only
- **(b) 1 and 3 only** ✓
- (c) 2 and 3 only
- (d) 1, 2 and 3
> **Explanation:** Teachings reached the common people, not just rulers. The teachings were spread through monks, scholars and pilgrims.

### Q4. What material was used to make India's first coins?
- (a) Gold
- (b) Bronze
- **(c) Silver** ✓
- (d) Copper
> **Explanation:** The first coins were punch-marked silver coins.

### Q5. Which metal became important during India's Second Urbanisation?
- (a) Tin
- **(b) Iron** ✓
- (c) Silver
- (d) Bronze
> **Explanation:** Iron tools boosted agriculture and warfare, supporting urban growth.

### Q6. What type of coins are called punch-marked coins?
- (a) Coins with royal portraits
- (b) Coins made of shells
- **(c) Coins with symbols stamped onto them** ✓
- (d) Coins used in religious rituals
> **Explanation:** Punch-marked coins had symbols punched or stamped onto the metal.

### Q7. Consider the following statements about the Varna system in ancient India:
1. The Varna system had four main groups: Brahmins, Kshatriyas, Vaishyas, and Shudras.
2. It was based on ideas from the Vedas.
3. People could never change their Varna under any circumstances.

Which of the above statements is/are correct?
- **(a) 1 and 2 only** ✓
- (b) 1 and 3 only
- (c) 2 and 3 only
- (d) 1, 2 and 3
> **Explanation:** Early Indian society allowed mobility between varnas (e.g. Brahmins taking up military roles); thus, statement 3 is incorrect.

### Q8. Which of the following correctly describes the role of different varnas?
1. Brahmins – priests, teachers, scholars
2. Kshatriyas – traders and merchants
3. Vaishyas – rulers and priests
4. Shudras – artisans and workers

Select the correct answer:
- **(a) 1 and 4 only** ✓
- (b) 1 and 2 only
- (c) 2 and 3 only
- (d) 1, 2, 3 and 4
> **Explanation:** Kshatriyas were warriors/rulers, not traders. Vaishyas were traders/farmers, not rulers.

### Q9. What were the early Indian assemblies like Sabha and Samiti mainly composed of?
- (a) Women and children
- (b) Military officers
- **(c) Elders and respected men** ✓
- (d) Traders only
> **Explanation:** These assemblies advised the king and were usually formed by elders.

### Q10. Consider the following statements about ancient Indian trade routes:
1. Uttarapatha connected the northwestern region with eastern India via the Ganga plains.
2. Dakshinapatha crossed the Vindhya hills to connect northern and southern India.
3. These routes had no connection to coastal ports.

Which of the above is/are correct?
- **(a) 1 and 2 only** ✓
- (b) 2 and 3 only
- (c) 1 and 3 only
- (d) 1, 2 and 3
> **Explanation:** Ports on both the coasts were connected to these routes; thus, statement 3 is incorrect.

### Q11. With reference to urbanisation in the 1st millennium BCE, consider the following statements:
1. Sisupalgarh in eastern India had wide roads and strong fortifications.
2. Cities in South India began emerging around 400 BCE.
3. The first urban centres in the south were built around the Indus River.

Which of the statements are correct?
- **(a) 1 and 2 only** ✓
- (b) 2 and 3 only
- (c) 1 and 3 only
- (d) 1, 2 and 3
> **Explanation:** Indus River is in the northwest, not the south. Thus, statement 3 is incorrect.

### Q12. Which kingdoms were formed in South India around 400 BCE?
- (a) Mauryas, Sungas, Guptas
- **(b) Cheras, Cholas, Pandyas** ✓
- (c) Kalinga, Magadha, Avanti
- (d) Satavahanas, Kushanas, Vakatakas
> **Explanation:** These were the early historic Tamil kingdoms of south India.

### Q13. What was a primary reason for South India's importance in early trade?
- (a) Harappan sites
- **(b) Rich resources like spices and gems** ✓
- (c) Silk weaving tradition
- (d) Presence of Vedic schools
> **Explanation:** Spices, gold, and gems made South India valuable in Indian and overseas trade.
