## Level 01 | Moderate

### Q1. What does the abbreviation "BC" stand for in historical timelines?
- (a) Before Century
- (b) Before Civilization
- **(c) Before Christ** ✓
- (d) Before Calendar
> **Explanation:** BC stands for "Before Christ" and is used to indicate years before the birth of Jesus Christ. Dates in BC are counted backwards, so a larger BC number means an earlier time.

### Q2. Which of the following is an example of a manuscript?
- (a) A stone pillar with royal orders
- **(b) A handwritten text on palm leaves** ✓
- (c) A painted cave wall
- (d) A fired clay tablet with city records
> **Explanation:** Manuscripts are handwritten texts, often found on materials like palm leaves, birch bark, or cloth. They are important sources of ancient history. Stone pillars with royal orders are inscriptions, not manuscripts.

### Q3. What is archaeology?
- (a) The study of old languages
- (b) The study of ancient coins
- **(c) The study of the past through physical remains and excavation** ✓
- (d) The study of ancient manuscripts
> **Explanation:** Archaeology is the scientific study of the human past through the excavation and analysis of physical remains such as tools, pottery, buildings, and bones. Archaeologists dig at sites to uncover evidence of past life.

### Q4. Which of the following is a primary source of history?
- (a) A modern history textbook
- (b) A documentary film about the Mauryan Empire
- **(c) An inscription carved on a rock by Emperor Ashoka** ✓
- (d) A biography of a historian
> **Explanation:** A primary source is one created at the time being studied. Ashoka's rock inscriptions were carved during his own reign (3rd century BCE), making them firsthand, contemporary evidence. Textbooks and documentaries are secondary sources.

### Q5. The term "AD" used in historical dates is an abbreviation for a Latin phrase. What does it mean?
- (a) After Death
- (b) After Decades
- **(c) Anno Domini, meaning "in the year of the Lord"** ✓
- (d) Ancient Date
> **Explanation:** AD stands for "Anno Domini," a Latin phrase meaning "in the year of the Lord." It refers to the years counted from the traditional birth year of Jesus Christ. It does not mean "After Death."

## Level 02 | Advanced

### Q1. Consider the following statements about inscriptions as historical sources:
**Statement I:** Inscriptions are written records carved on hard surfaces like stone, metal, or pillars.
**Statement II:** Inscriptions can only be found in Sanskrit language in ancient India.
Which of the following is correct?
- **(a) Statement I is correct but Statement II is incorrect** ✓
- (b) Both Statement I and Statement II are correct
- (c) Statement I is incorrect but Statement II is correct
- (d) Both Statement I and Statement II are incorrect
> **Explanation:** Inscriptions are indeed carved on hard surfaces and are a key historical source. However, they were written in many languages — Ashoka's inscriptions, for example, were written in Prakrit, Greek, and Aramaic, not just Sanskrit.

### Q2. Assertion (A): Historians prefer primary sources over secondary sources when studying ancient periods.
Reason (R): Primary sources are created close to the events being studied and offer direct, firsthand evidence.
- (a) Both A and R are correct, but R is not the correct explanation of A
- **(b) Both A and R are correct, and R is the correct explanation of A** ✓
- (c) A is correct but R is incorrect
- (d) A is incorrect but R is correct
> **Explanation:** Primary sources — such as inscriptions, coins, manuscripts, and archaeological finds — are created during or close to the time period under study. This makes them more directly reliable than secondary sources, which are later interpretations, and historians therefore give them greater weight.

### Q3. Which of the following pairs of historical source and its category is INCORRECTLY matched?
- (a) Palm leaf manuscript — Literary source
- (b) Ashoka's rock edict — Epigraphic source
- **(c) Ancient coins — Archaeological excavation** ✓
- (d) Pottery fragments — Archaeological source
> **Explanation:** Ancient coins are classified as numismatic sources, a distinct category within material culture, not specifically as "archaeological excavation" (which refers to the process of digging). Coins are studied through numismatics. While they may be found during excavations, the category name for coin study is numismatics.

### Q4. Consider the following sources of history:
1. Coins
2. Manuscripts
3. Inscriptions
4. Oral traditions

Which of these are considered "material sources"?
- (a) 1 and 2 only
- (b) 2 and 3 only
- **(c) 1, 3, and 4** ✓
- (d) 1 only
> **Explanation:** Material sources are physical, tangible objects from the past. Coins and inscriptions are material sources. Oral traditions, while not physical objects, are sometimes grouped with material/non-written sources. Manuscripts are written (literary) sources, not material sources in the strict sense.

### Q5. Why is it important to use multiple sources to reconstruct history?
- (a) Because a single source is always incorrect
- (b) Because multiple sources make history more interesting to read
- **(c) Because different sources can verify each other and fill gaps that a single source may leave** ✓
- (d) Because historians are required by law to cite at least three sources
> **Explanation:** No single source tells the complete story of the past. Each type of source — literary, epigraphic, archaeological, numismatic — provides different kinds of information. Using multiple sources allows historians to cross-check evidence, identify biases, and construct a more accurate and complete picture of history.

## Level 03 | Previous Years

### Q1. Which of the following correctly explains the difference between BC and AD in the historical timeline?
- (a) BC dates are always smaller numbers than AD dates
- (b) BC refers to years after a particular event, while AD refers to years before it
- **(c) BC dates count backwards from a reference point, while AD dates count forwards from the same reference point** ✓
- (d) BC and AD refer to different geographical regions' calendars
> **Explanation:** The reference point is the traditional birth year of Jesus Christ. BC (Before Christ) dates count backwards — so 500 BC is earlier than 100 BC. AD (Anno Domini) dates count forwards. This means the timeline goes: 500 BC → 100 BC → 1 AD → 500 AD, with larger BC numbers being older.

### Q2. What kind of historical information can coins provide to historians?
- (a) Only the names of kings who ruled
- (b) Only the economic conditions of a period
- **(c) Information about rulers, trade, religious beliefs, and the prosperity of a kingdom** ✓
- (d) Coins cannot provide reliable historical information
> **Explanation:** Ancient coins are rich historical sources. They can tell us about the rulers who issued them (names, titles, portraits), the symbols of religious or cultural importance at the time, the extent of trade networks (based on where coins are found), and the economic strength of a kingdom.

### Q3. Manuscripts written on birch bark and palm leaves have survived for centuries. What is the PRIMARY reason historians must treat these sources with caution?
- (a) They were written in languages no one can read today
- (b) They are too fragile to be studied directly
- **(c) They may contain copying errors introduced over centuries of manual reproduction** ✓
- (d) They were all written by foreign travelers and may be biased
> **Explanation:** Manuscripts were copied by hand over generations. Each time a scribe copied a text, errors could be introduced — accidentally or intentionally. This means the version we have today may differ from the original. Historians must compare multiple copies and use critical analysis to identify the most accurate version.

### Q4. A student finds a list of dates: 3000 BC, 1500 BC, 500 BC, 200 AD, 1000 AD. Which of the following correctly arranges these from the OLDEST to the MOST RECENT?
- (a) 200 AD → 500 BC → 1500 BC → 3000 BC → 1000 AD
- **(b) 3000 BC → 1500 BC → 500 BC → 200 AD → 1000 AD** ✓
- (c) 1000 AD → 200 AD → 500 BC → 1500 BC → 3000 BC
- (d) 500 BC → 200 AD → 1500 BC → 3000 BC → 1000 AD
> **Explanation:** BC dates are counted backwards from the birth of Christ — so 3000 BC is the furthest in the past. AD dates count forward. The correct order from oldest to most recent is: 3000 BC → 1500 BC → 500 BC → 200 AD → 1000 AD.

### Q5. Which of the following statements about the work of a historian is most accurate?
- (a) Historians only study written records and ignore all other forms of evidence
- (b) Historians accept all ancient sources as completely truthful
- **(c) Historians critically analyze various types of sources to construct an account of the past** ✓
- (d) Historians focus only on events from the last 500 years
> **Explanation:** A historian's work involves gathering evidence from many types of sources — written, epigraphic, archaeological, numismatic, and oral — and critically evaluating them for bias, accuracy, and context. They do not accept sources uncritically, and they study all time periods, not just recent history.
