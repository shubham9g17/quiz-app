## Level 01 | Moderate

### Q1. How much of the produce was collected as a tax?
- (a) One-third part
- (b) One-fifth part
- **(c) One-sixth part** ✓
- (d) Half part
> **Explanation:** Taxes on crops were collected from people because most people were farmers. Usually, the tax was fixed at one-sixth of what was produced. This was known as bhaga or a share.

### Q2. Magadha and __________ are examples of monarchical mahajanapadas.
- (a) Shakyas
- (b) Vatsa
- **(c) Vajji** ✓
- (d) Lichchavis
> **Explanation:** The Mahajanapadas were either monarchical or republican in character. Examples of monarchical mahajanapadas are: Magadha: Between Anga and Vatsa lay the kingdom of Magadha, corresponding to modern Patna and Gaya districts, bounded on the north and west by the rivers Ganga and Son, on the south by the Vindhya outcrop and on the east by the river Champa. Vatsa: The Vatsa country had a monarchical form of government. Its capital was Kaushambi (identified with the village of Kosam, 38 miles from Allahabad). Kaushambi, a very prosperous city, was the most important entrepôt of goods and passengers from the south and the west.

### Q3. How did men become rulers around 3,000 years ago?

### Q6. In the context of the Vedic period, consider the following statements:
1. Both the Kshatriyas and the Vaishyas could perform sacrifices.
2. Shudras were considered to be untouchable.
3. All the groups were decided on the basis of birth.

Which of the statement(s) given above is/are correct?
- (a) 1 only
- (b) 2 only
- **(c) 1 and 3 only** ✓
- (d) 1, 2 and 3 only
> **Explanation:** Statement 1 is correct. In Vedic times, both Kshatriya and Vaishya had the right to sacrifice. Statement 2 is incorrect. The concept of untouchables came after the Vedic period. Until the Vedic period, the Shudras were not considered untouchable. Later, some crafts-persons, hunters and gatherers, as well as people who helped perform burials and cremations were considered untouchable. Statement 3 is correct. By the end of the Vedic period, the priests also said that these groups were decided on the basis of birth. For example, if one's father and mother were Brahmins, one would automatically become a Brahmin and so on.

### Q9. With respect to the major changes in agricultural sector in the Mahajanapada time, consider the following questions:
1. The use of iron ploughshares grew.
2. People began transplanting paddy.

Which of the statement(s) given above is/are correct?
- (a) 1 only
- (b) 2 only
- **(c) Both 1 and 2** ✓
- (d) Neither 1 nor 2
> **Explanation:** Both the statements are correct. There were two major changes in agriculture around this time: One was the growing use of iron ploughshares. This meant that heavy, clayey soil could be turned over better than with a wooden ploughshare, so that more grain could be produced. Second, people began transplanting paddy. This meant that instead of scattering seed on the ground, from which plants would sprout, seedlings were grown and then planted in the fields. This led to increased production, as many more plants survived.
