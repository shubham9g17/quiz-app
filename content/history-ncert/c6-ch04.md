## Level 01 | Moderate

### Q1. Which of the following is the oldest Veda?
- (a) Samaveda
- (b) Yajurveda
- **(c) Rigveda** ✓
- (d) Atharvaveda
> **Explanation:** The oldest Veda is Rigveda, composed about 3,500 years ago. The Rigveda includes more than a thousand hymns, called suktas or "well-said lines." These hymns are in praise of various gods and goddesses.

### Q2. Who is the god of fire according to Rigveda?
- **(a) Agni** ✓
- (b) Indra
- (c) Soma
- (d) None of these
> **Explanation:** Agni, the God of Fire, is one of the most prominent of the deities of the Vedas. Fire is central to all Vedic rituals. All the offerings in the Vedic sacrifices are invariably offered to Agni and through him to other gods. Agni is thus the primary recipient of all Vedic sacrifices.

### Q3. In the Vedic age, Indra was god of:
- (a) Wind
- (b) Eternity
- **(c) Rain and Thunder (warrior god)** ✓
- (d) Fire
> **Explanation:** The time period of the Vedic age is 1500 BC-1100 BC. During this age, Indra was the God of Rain and Thunder (warrior god). Indra was the most important god in the Vedic religion and he later became a major figure in Hindu Dharma and an important deity in Buddhism, Cham and Chinese tradition.

### Q4. In which language is Rigveda written?
- **(a) Vedic Sanskrit** ✓
- (b) Vedic Hindi
- (c) Vedic Tamil
- (d) None of these
> **Explanation:** The Rig veda is the earliest of the four Vedas and one of the most important texts of the Hindu tradition. It is a large collection of hymns in praise of the gods, which are chanted in various rituals. They were composed in an archaic language named Vedic Sanskrit, that gradually evolved into classical Sanskrit.

### Q5. In the Rigveda, the terms Dasas and Dasyus refer to:
- (a) Robbers
- (b) Tribals
- **(c) Non-Aryans** ✓
- (d) Menials
> **Explanation:** The Rigveda contains accounts of conflicts between the Aryas and the Dasas and Dasyus. It describes Dasas and Dasyus as people who do not perform sacrifices (akratu) or obey the commandments of gods (avrata). Dasas and Dasyus were early Indo-Aryan immigrants who arrived into the subcontinent before the Vedic Aryans.

### Q6. Consider the following statements about Rigveda:
1. The oldest Veda is the Rigveda, composed about 3,500 years ago.
2. The Rigveda includes more than a thousand hymns, called suktas or 'well-said lines'. These hymns are in praise of various rajas and kings.

Which of the above statements is/are correct?
- **(a) 1 only** ✓
- (b) 2 only
- (c) Both 1 and 2
- (d) Neither 1 nor 2
> **Explanation:** There are four Vedas: the Rigveda, the Samaveda, the Yajurveda and the Atharvaveda. Of these, the Rigveda is the oldest and was composed 3,500 years ago. The Rigveda includes more than a thousand hymns, called suktas or "well-said lines". These hymns are in praise of various gods and goddesses. Three gods are especially important: Agni, the god of fire; Indra, a warrior god; and Soma, a plant from which a special drink was prepared. Hence, Statement 2 is not correct.

### Q7. Inamgaon is situated on which of the following rivers?
- (a) Son
- (b) Narmada
- (c) Bhima
- **(d) Ghod** ✓
> **Explanation:** Inamgaon is a post-Harappan agrarian village and archaeological site located in Maharashtra, western India. Situated along the right bank of the Ghod River, it is considered to be the regional centre of the Bhima Valley.

### Q8. Who among the following was an expert in medicine?
- (a) Banabhatta
- (b) Ashvaghosha
- **(c) Charaka** ✓
- (d) Satakarni
> **Explanation:** Charaka was one of the principal contributors to the ancient art and science of Ayurveda, a system of medicine and lifestyle developed in Ancient India. Charaka has been identified as a native of Kashmir. He is well-known as the "father of medicine".

### Q9. Agriculture and ______ were the main occupations of Aryans.
- (a) Jewellery-making
- **(b) Cattle-rearing** ✓
- (c) Metal-work
- (d) Pottery-making
> **Explanation:** The Aryans were essentially pastoral people. Their main occupation was cattle rearing.

### Q10. On which barks was the Rig Veda written?
- (a) Neem Bark
- (b) Cinchona Bark
- (c) Tulsi Bark
- **(d) Birch Bark** ✓
> **Explanation:** One manuscript was written on birch bark in ancient Sharada script, while the remaining 29 manuscripts were written in Devanagari script. The oldest manuscript dates back to 1464 AD, and the manuscripts had several unique features in terms of scripts, accentuation marks and support material use. Birch bark is the bark of Paper Birch Tree and was used as a writing material from prehistoric times.

### Q11. The evidence of which fruits were found in Inamgaon.
- (a) Ber, Amla, Jamun
- (b) Mango
- (c) Apple
- **(d) All of these** ✓
> **Explanation:** Several fruits were consumed by the people of Inamgaon, namely dates, Jamun amla and ber.

### Q12. When the dead body was buried with gold beads, it meant that the dead man was a ______.
- **(a) rich man** ✓
- (b) poor man
- (c) sage
- (d) follower
> **Explanation:** Rich people were buried with more gold beads, stone beads, copper bangles, etc. Whereas poor people were buried with only a few pots.

### Q2. In the context of the Rigveda, consider the following statements:
1. All the Suktas in the Rigveda were composed by men.
2. The language of the Rigveda is called Sanskrit or Vedic Sanskrit.
3. The Rigveda was recited and heard rather than read.

How many of the statements given above is/are correct?
- (a) Only one
- **(b) Only two** ✓
- (c) All three
- (d) None
> **Explanation:** Statement 1 is not correct because these hymns were composed by sages (Rishis). Priests taught students to recite and memorise each syllable, word and sentence with great care. Most of the hymns were composed, taught and learned by men. A few were composed by women. Statement 2 is correct. The language of the Rigveda is known as Vedic Sanskrit, which is distinct from Classical Sanskrit. Statement 3 is correct. The Rig Veda was originally recited and heard, rather than read. It was passed down orally for centuries before being written down.

- (d) Mathematics
> **Explanation:** About 2,000 years ago, there was a famous physician named Charaka who wrote a book on medicine known as the Charaka Samhita. There he states that the human body has 360 bones. ### Q4. Which of the following statements is NOT correct about the Rigveda?
- **(a) The Rigveda mentions Varna** ✓
- (b) Slavery was not mentioned in the Rigveda
- (c) Sabha the assembly was mentioned in the Rigveda
- (d) Rajan occurs many times in the Rigveda
> **Explanation:** The Rigveda is the oldest religious text in the world. It is the collection of hymns, composed around 1700 BC, containing 1,028 hymns and is divided into 10 mandals. The 10th Mandala of the Rigveda contains the Purushasukta hymn, which tells about the origin of the caste system. The Rigvedic society comprised four varnas, namely Brahmana, Kshatriya, Vaishya and Shudra. Slavery was mentioned in the Rigveda. It was mentioned in the term Dasa. Three to four verses of the Rigveda mention Dasas. Hence, Statement (b) is not correct. Sabha, the assembly, was mentioned in the Rigveda. The term Sabha means both the assembly and the assembly hall. The word Rajan was used for a king (Raja). It occurs many times in the Rigveda.

### Q7. Consider the following statements:

2. The Upanishads were composed earlier than the Puranas.

Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

- **(b) 2 only** ✓
- (c) Both 1 and 2
- (d) Neither 1 nor 2

Some Upanishads include parables. For example, the wise beggar was a dialogue based on a story from one of the most famous Upanishads, the 'Chhandogya Upanishad' and the anthology of the two birds was mentioned in the 'Katopanishad'.
