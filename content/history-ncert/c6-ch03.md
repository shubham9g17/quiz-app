## Level 01 | Moderate

### Q1. When did Harappan cities develop?
- **(a) About 4,700 years ago** ✓
- (b) About 3,700 years ago
- (c) About 2,700 years ago
- (d) About 1,700 years ago
> **Explanation:** The content states: "These cities developed about 4,700 years ago." Harappa was excavated by Daya Ram Sahani in 1921, and the civilisation is dated to approximately 2700–1900 BCE, roughly 4,700 years ago.

### Q2. A place where surplus grain was stored was known as:
- (a) Storage
- (b) Warehouse
- (c) Basket
- **(d) Granaries** ✓
> **Explanation:** Granaries were large structures used to store surplus grain in Harappan cities. Mohenjo-Daro, Harappa and Lothal had elaborate store-houses (granaries).

### Q3. Which of the following was the first city to be discovered in the Indian subcontinent?
- **(a) Harappa** ✓
- (b) Mohenjo-Daro
- (c) Lothal
- (d) Sotka Koh
> **Explanation:** Harappa was the first city to be discovered in the Indian subcontinent. It was first identified by a British railway explorer in 1826 and later excavated by Daya Ram Sahani in 1921.

### Q4. What was the colour of the faience?
- (a) Red
- **(b) Blue or sea green** ✓
- (c) Yellow
- (d) Black
> **Explanation:** Faience is a glazed non-clay ceramic material. Harappan faience was typically blue or sea green in colour, used to make beads, bangles, and other decorative objects.

### Q5. How many storeys of houses were generally found in Harappan cities?
- **(a) One or two storeys** ✓
- (b) Four to five storeys
- (c) Multi-storeyed
- (d) None of these
> **Explanation:** The content states: "Houses were either one or two storeys high, with a separate bathing area and some had wells to supply water."

### Q6. The Great bath was found at which site of the Harappan Civilisation?
- (a) Harappa
- (b) Dholavira
- (c) Lothal
- **(d) Mohenjo-Daro** ✓
> **Explanation:** The content explicitly states: "The famous Great Bath was found in Mohenjo-Daro. The important people took a dip in this tank on special occasions." Steps led down from two sides, with rooms on all sides.

### Q7. In the context of the cities of the Harappan civilisation, which of the following statements is not correct?
- (a) Many of these cities were divided into two or more parts.
- **(b) The walls of raw bricks were built around each part.** ✓
- (c) Archaeologists called the lower part of the city as the lower town and the higher part as the citadel.
- (d) Usually, the western was smaller but higher, and the eastern was larger but lower.
> **Explanation:** Statement (b) is incorrect. The content clearly states that buildings and walls were "made of uniformly baked bricks," not raw (unbaked) bricks. Baked bricks were laid in an interlocking pattern to make walls strong.

### Q8. Who supplied food to craft-persons, scribes and rulers in the cities?
- (a) Local citizens
- **(b) Farmers and herders** ✓
- (c) Postman
- (d) All of these
> **Explanation:** Craft-persons, scribes and rulers in cities were supplied food by farmers and herders who lived in villages around the cities. This surplus food supported the urban population.

### Q9. What was used to dig the earth for turning the soil and planting seeds?
- (a) Digger
- (b) Axe
- **(c) Plough** ✓
- (d) Plaster
> **Explanation:** The content states: "The plough was used to dig the earth for turning the soil and planting seeds." The Harappans also developed new tools known as ploughs used for this purpose.

### Q10. At which of the following Harappan sites was a dockyard found?
- **(a) Lothal** ✓
- (b) Harappa
- (c) Chanhudaro
- (d) Mohenjo-Daro
> **Explanation:** The content states: "Coastal settlements like Lothal had large dockyards for handling goods." Lothal was a port town on the Gujarat coast, strategically located near the Sabarmati River and Gulf of Khambhat.

### Q11. Where is Mehrgarh located today?
- (a) Uttar Pradesh
- (b) Andhra Pradesh
- **(c) Pakistan** ✓
- (d) China
> **Explanation:** Mehrgarh is one of the oldest known farming settlements in South Asia, located in what is today the Balochistan province of Pakistan. It dates back to approximately 7000 BCE and is considered a precursor to the Harappan civilisation.

### Q5. Which among the following is the most unique feature of the Dholavira site of the Indus Valley Civilisation?
- (a) Its cities were horizontally divided into multiple parts
- (b) Its cities were vertically divided
- **(c) Its cities were divided into three parts** ✓
- (d) None of these
> **Explanation:** Dholavira is unique because it was divided into three distinct parts — the upper town (citadel), middle town, and lower town — unlike most Harappan cities that had only two divisions (citadel and lower town). The diagram in the chapter specifically highlights this three-part division as a distinctive feature.
