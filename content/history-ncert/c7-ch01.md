## Level 01 | Moderate

### Q1. Cartographer is a person who makes:
- (a) Cartoons
- (b) Caricatures
- **(c) Maps** ✓
- (d) None of these
> **Explanation:** A cartographer is a person who creates maps, whether they're of the world, the local bus routes or buried pirate treasure.

### Q2. Why do historians today have to be careful about the terms in the past?
- (a) Because they do not know the meaning of the terms.
- (b) Because the terms may have more than one meaning.
- **(c) Because the terms had different meanings in the past.** ✓
- (d) None of the above
> **Explanation:** Historians today have to be careful because the terms they use today meant different things in the past. For example, a simple term like "foreigner" is used today to mean someone who is not an Indian. In the medieval period, a "foreigner" was any stranger who appeared, say in a given village, and someone who was not a part of that society or culture.

### Q3. The number and variety of textual records increase dramatically during the period between:
- (a) 600 – 700
- (b) 700 – 800
- (c) 800 – 1000
- **(d) 700 – 1750** ✓
> **Explanation:** The number and variety of textual records increased dramatically during the period of 700 to 1750, slowly displacing other types of available information.

### Q4. Who used to collect manuscripts?
- (a) Poor people
- **(b) Wealthy people** ✓
- (c) Local people
- (d) None of these
> **Explanation:** Manuscripts were collected by wealthy people, rulers, monasteries and temples. They were placed in libraries and archives.

### Q5. Ziyauddin Barani wrote his first chronicle in:
- (a) 13th century
- (b) 12th century
- **(c) 14th century** ✓
- (d) 15th century
> **Explanation:** The fourteenth-century chronicler, Ziyauddin Barani, wrote his chronicle first in 1356 and another version two years later. The two differ from each other, but historians did not know about the existence of the first version until the 1960s.

### Q6. When did the Arab geographer Al-Idrisi made the map of the world?
- (a) 1177 CE
- **(b) 1154 CE** ✓
- (c) 1274 CE
- (d) 1264 CE
> **Explanation:** The correct answer is 1154 CE. Arab geographer Al-Idrisi drew a section of the world map in the twelfth century showing the Indian subcontinent from land to sea.

### Q7. In which century did Babur used Hindustan to describe the geography of subcontinent?
- (a) Seventeenth century
- (b) Eighteenth century
- **(c) Sixteenth century** ✓
- (d) None of these
> **Explanation:** In the early sixteenth century, Babur used Hindustan to describe the geography, the fauna and the culture of the inhabitants of the subcontinent.

### Q8. Which language was spoken during the Fourteenth century in the present Andhra Pradesh region?
- (a) Awadhi
- (b) Gauri
- **(c) Telugu** ✓
- (d) Lahori
> **Explanation:** In 1318, the poet Amir Khusrau noted that there were different languages in every region of this land: Sindhi, Lahori, Kashmiri, Dvarsamudri, Telugu (in southern Karnataka), Ma'bari (in Andhra Pradesh), etc.

## Level 02 | Advanced

### Q1. Areas of Punjab, Haryana and the lands between the Ganga and Yamuna – came under _____.
- (a) Turks
- **(b) Delhi Sultanate** ✓
- (c) Sultan of Mysore
- (d) Mughals
> **Explanation:** In the thirteenth century, the term "Hindustan" meant the areas of Punjab, Haryana and the land between the Ganga and Yamuna. The term was used in the political sense for lands that were a part of the dominions of the Delhi Sultanate.

### Q2. Between 700 and 1750, one group of people who became important was the _____.
- (a) Marathas
- **(b) Rajputs** ✓
- (c) Mughals
- (d) British
> **Explanation:** Rajput's name derived from "Rajaputra, the son of a ruler", and became quite popular during the period. Between the eighth and the fourteenth centuries, the term was given to a group of warriors, claiming Kshatriya caste status, including not just rulers and chieftains but also soldiers and commanders who served in the armies of different monarchs all over the subcontinent.

### Q3. For whom was the term "foreigner" used in the medieval period?
1. Any person coming to the village who was a stranger.
2. People of different religions used this term for each other in the same village.

Which of the above statements is/are true?
- (a) 1 only
- (b) 2 only
- **(c) Both 1 and 2** ✓
- (d) Neither 1 nor 2
> **Explanation:** In the medieval period, any unknown person who came to a village and was not a part of that society or culture was called a 'foreigner'. Such a person is called a 'Vilayati' in Hindi and 'Ajnabi' in Persian, but two farmers living in the same village were not foreigners for each other, even if they belonged to different religions or castes.

### Q4. Consider the following statements:
1. When Minhaj-i-Siraj used the word 'Hindustan' for the first time, he meant it for the areas located in undivided India.
2. Babur used the term 'Hindustan' to describe the geography, the fauna and the culture of the inhabitants of the subcontinent.

Which of the above statements is/are true?
- (a) 1 only
- **(b) 2 only** ✓
- (c) Both 1 and 2
- (d) Neither 1 nor 2
> **Explanation:** Minhaj-i-Siraj used the term 'Hindustan' in the thirteenth century. In that era, it meant the areas of Punjab, Haryana and the lands between the Ganga and Yamuna. He used the term in a political sense for lands that were a part of the dominions of the Delhi Sultanate. The areas included in the term 'Hindustan' didn't even the extent of the Sultanate, but the term never included South India. In contrast, in the early sixteenth century, Babur used the word 'Hindustan' to describe the geography, the fauna and the culture of the inhabitants of the subcontinent.

### Q5. Consider the following statements:
1. In the early sixteenth century, Babur used Hindustan to describe the geography of the inhabitants of the subcontinent.
2. In the fourteenth century, the poet Amir Khusrau used the word "Hind".
3. Before the fourteenth century, the idea of a geographical and cultural entity like "Hindustan" did not exist.

Which of the above statements is/are true?
- (a) 1 and 2 only
- (b) 2 and 3 only
- **(c) 1 and 3 only** ✓
- (d) 1, 2 and 3
> **Explanation:** In the early sixteenth century, Babur used Hindustan to describe the geography, the fauna and the culture of the inhabitants of the subcontinent. Amir Khusrau used the word "Hind". Hence, statements 1 and 2 are correct. Even if the idea of a geographical and cultural entity like "India" did exist, the term "Hindustan" did not carry the political and national meanings associated with it today. Hence, statement 3 is not correct.

### Q6. With reference to certain key historical terms used in medieval and early modern India, consider the following statements:
1. Manuscripts were printed texts that served as primary sources of historical information.
2. Jati was a caste-based system that determined a person's social ranking.
3. Periodisation helps in the systematic study of history by dividing it into chronological segments.
4. In the medieval period, a foreigner was anyone who did not belong to a particular society or village.

Which of the statements given above is/are correct?
- **(a) 2, 3 and 4 only** ✓
- (b) 1, 2 and 3 only
- (c) 1 and 4 only
- (d) 2 and 3 only
> **Explanation:** Statement 1 is incorrect: Manuscripts were handwritten, not printed. They were often written on palm leaves, bark, or paper and used as important sources of historical knowledge. Statement 2 is correct: Jati or caste defined a person's place in the social hierarchy, which influenced their occupation, status, and societal interactions. Statement 3 is correct: Periodisation refers to dividing history into time-based segments, helping historians analyze and understand developments and patterns over time. Statement 4 is correct: In medieval India, the term foreigner had a more localized meaning and referred to someone from outside the village or cultural group, not necessarily someone from another country.

### Q7. In the context of Historians and their sources, consider the following statements:
1. Historians rely on the coins, inscriptions, architecture and textual records for historical information.
2. The significance of textual records decreased during the medieval period.
3. Paper was used to write holy texts, chronicles of rulers, letters and teachings of saints.

Which of the above statements is/are true?
- (a) 1 and 2 only
- (b) 2 and 3 only
- **(c) 1 and 3 only** ✓
- (d) 1, 2 and 3
> **Explanation:** Historians rely on coins, inscriptions, architecture and textual records for historical information, but the significance of textual records has increased during this period. Hence, statement 1 is correct, but statement 2 is incorrect. People used paper to write holy texts, chronicles of rulers, letters and teachings of saints, petitions and judicial records and registers of accounts and taxes. Hence, statement 3 is correct.

### Q8. In the medieval period, what purpose did the "Persian Wheel" serve?
- **(a) Irrigation** ✓
- (b) Spinning yarn
- (c) Making ammunition weapons
- (d) Making earthen items
> **Explanation:** The study of the 1000 years spanning between 700 and 1750 is a challenge. The scale and variety of developments that occurred over the period are huge. At different moments in this period, new technologies made their appearances – like the Persian wheel in irrigation.

### Q10. Consider the following statements:
1. Many forest-dwellers migrated between the eighth and the fourteenth centuries due to changes in habitat.
2. There was a gradual clearing of forests and the extension of agriculture, faster and more complete in some areas than in others.

Which of the above statements is/are true?
- (a) 1 only
- (b) 2 only
- **(c) Both 1 and 2** ✓
- (d) Neither 1 nor 2
> **Explanation:** Between the eighth and the fourteenth centuries, many forest-dwellers migrated due to changes in habitat. There was a gradual clearing of forests and the extension of agriculture, faster and more complete in some areas than in others. Hence both the statements are correct. Those who remained, started to till their lands and became peasants, who were later influenced by the regional markets, chieftains, priests, monasteries and temples.

## Level 03 | Previous Years

### Q1. Consider the following statements with regard to Brahmans and choose the wrong statement:
- (a) Brahmanas earned respect in the society for their knowledge of Sanskrit texts.
- (b) Temples were constructed by royalty and the importance of Brahmans was increased.
- (c) A major development by the Brahmans was the emergence of the idea of bhakti.
- **(d) The royal patrons did not follow Brahmanas, they had their own religious leaders.** ✓
> **Explanation:** Between the eighth and the eighteenth centuries important changes occurred in Hinduism including the worship of new deities, the construction of temples by royalty and the growing importance of Brahmanas, the priests, as dominant groups in society. Brahmanas earned respect in the society for their knowledge of Sanskrit texts and their dominant position was consolidated by the support of their patrons—new rulers searching for prestige. Hence, statement (d) is incorrect. Another major development of this period was the emergence of the idea of bhakti – a loving, personal deity that devotees could reach without the aid of priests or elaborate rituals.

- (a) Old Age, Middle Age and New Age
- **(b) Hindus, Muslims and British** ✓
- (c) Hindus, Muslims and Christians
- (d) Ancient, Medieval and Modern
> **Explanation:** In the middle of the nineteenth century, British historians divided the history of India into three periods (based on religion): 1. Hindu 2. Muslim 3. British

- **(b) Punjab, Haryana and the land between Ganga and Yamuna** ✓
- (c) Central India and Deccan
- (d) Punjab and Sindh
> **Explanation:** "Hindustan" is known as "India," in the modern nation-state of today. The term was first used in the thirteenth century by Minhaj-i-Siraj, a chronicler who wrote in Persian and contains the areas of Punjab, Haryana and the land between the Ganga and Yamuna. The term was used in a political sense for lands that were a part of the dominions of the Delhi Sultan.
